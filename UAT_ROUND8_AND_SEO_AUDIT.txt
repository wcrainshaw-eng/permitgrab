EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
The site has a STRONG technical SEO foundation — proper canonicals,

✓ Every page has unique <title> and meta description
✓ Canonical URLs correctly set on all pages tested

✓ Twitter card meta tags present

✓ Sitemap at /sitemap.xml with 2,078 URLs, zero duplicates
✓ City pages have 4 schema types: Dataset, BreadcrumbList, WebPage, FAQPage

--- B. CRITICAL SEO ISSUES (fix immediately) ---

B1. 168 BLOG POSTS MISSING FROM SITEMAP [CRITICAL]
    Blog page shows 252 articles. Sitemap contains only 84 blog URLs.
    That's 168 blog posts Google may not know about.
    Impact: Those posts won't get crawled/indexed efficiently.
    FIX: Ensure ALL published blog posts are included in sitemap.xml.
    This alone could double your indexed content overnight.

B2. BLOG POSTS MISSING article:published_time [HIGH]
    Blog posts have Article schema but no published date meta tag.
    Google uses freshness signals to rank content — "2026 guide" in
    the title means nothing if Google can't verify the publish date.
    FIX: Add <meta property="article:published_time" content="...">
    and <meta property="article:modified_time"> to all blog posts.
    Also add datePublished/dateModified to the Article schema JSON-LD.

B3. H1 MISSING SPACE BEFORE "&" ON ALL CITY PAGES [HIGH]
    H1 reads "Houston, TX Building Permits& Contractor Leads"
    Google uses H1 for ranking signals. A malformed H1 on ~848 city
    pages is a sitewide issue.
    FIX: Template fix — add space before "&" in city page H1.

B4. BLOG POSTS ARE TOO THIN [HIGH]
    Houston guide: 883 words. For a "complete contractor's guide"
    targeting "houston building permits" — a competitive keyword —
    this is thin. Top-ranking guides for city permit queries tend
    to be 1,500-3,000+ words.
    FIX: Expand city guide blog posts to 1,500-2,500 words with:
    - Specific permit fee schedules
    - Processing timeline data
    - Links to local building dept resources
    - Neighborhood-level construction trends
    - Trade-specific sections (plumbing, electrical, roofing)


--- C. HIGH-PRIORITY SEO IMPROVEMENTS ---

C1. STATE HUB PAGES NEED MORE SCHEMA MARKUP
    City pages have 4 schema types (Dataset, BreadcrumbList, WebPage, FAQPage).
    State hub pages only have CollectionPage.
    Missing: BreadcrumbList, FAQPage, WebPage schemas.
    FIX: Add BreadcrumbList schema to state hubs (they already have
    visual breadcrumbs). Add FAQ section + FAQPage schema for
    "building permits in [state]" queries.

C2. NO TRADE-SPECIFIC LANDING PAGES
    You have 15 trade categories (Plumbing, Electrical, HVAC, Roofing,
    Solar, etc.) but NO dedicated landing pages like:
    /permits/houston/plumbing or /trades/plumbing
    These would target high-intent searches like:
    - "plumbing permits houston"
    - "roofing permit leads texas"
    - "HVAC contractor leads near me"
    FIX: Create trade-specific pages for top cities. Even 50 pages
    (top 10 cities x 5 trades) would capture significant long-tail traffic.
    Add these to the sitemap.

C3. CITY PAGES NEED MORE UNIQUE CONTENT
    City pages have permit data + a short "About [City] Building Permits"
    section, but the content is template-driven and thin.
    Google's Helpful Content system penalizes pages that feel auto-generated.
    FIX: Add unique content per city:
    - Local building department contact info + hours
    - Average permit processing times for that city
    - Top neighborhoods by permit activity
    - Year-over-year permit trend charts
    - Local contractor licensing requirements

C4. NO INTERNAL LINKING FROM BLOG TO CITY PAGES
    Blog posts about Houston should link to /permits/houston.
    City pages link to blog posts, but cross-linking should go both ways.
    FIX: Add contextual internal links from blog posts to relevant
    city landing pages. This passes PageRank and helps Google understand
    topic clusters.

C5. MAP PAGE META DESC STALE
    Says "140+ US cities" — should be "840+ US cities" to match reality.
    FIX: Update meta description on /map route.


--- D. MEDIUM-PRIORITY SEO IMPROVEMENTS ---

D1. ADD LOCAL BUSINESS SCHEMA TO CITY PAGES
    For "building permits [city]" searches, adding LocalBusiness or
    GovernmentService schema referencing the local building department
    could improve visibility in local search results.

D2. ADD CITY-SPECIFIC FAQ CONTENT
    City pages have FAQPage schema but I didn't verify the FAQ content
    quality. FAQs should target real questions like:
    - "How long does a building permit take in Houston?"
    - "How much does a permit cost in Houston TX?"
    - "Do I need a permit for a fence in Houston?"
    These exact queries show up in Google's "People Also Ask" boxes.

D3. CONTRACTORS PAGE HAS NO SCHEMA
    /contractors has zero structured data markup.
    FIX: Add CollectionPage or Dataset schema.

D4. GET ALERTS PAGE HAS NO SCHEMA
    /get-alerts has no structured data.
    FIX: Add WebPage schema at minimum.

D5. ADD PRECONNECT/PRELOAD HINTS
    No <link rel="preconnect"> or <link rel="preload"> found.
    Adding preconnect for fonts/CDN resources improves Core Web Vitals.

D6. BLOG INDEX SHOWS 252 POSTS ON ONE PAGE
    Loading 252 articles on a single /blog page is slow and not
    SEO-friendly. Google prefers paginated or categorized blog indexes.
    FIX: Paginate to 20-30 posts per page, or add category filters
    (city guides, trade guides, market reports, how-to guides).
