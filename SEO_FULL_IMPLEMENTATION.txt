PERMITGRAB — FULL SEO IMPLEMENTATION PLAN
==========================================
Date: March 26, 2026
Goal: Every contractor searching for permits, leads, or construction
      activity should find PermitGrab on page 1.

==========================================
WHO'S SEARCHING AND WHAT THEY TYPE
==========================================

Your subscribers fall into 5 segments. Each searches differently:

SEGMENT 1: HUNGRY CONTRACTORS (highest intent — these convert)
  They just started their business or hit a dry spell. They're
  actively looking for new work RIGHT NOW.
  Searches:
    "construction leads near me"
    "contractor leads [city]"
    "how to find construction jobs"
    "building permit leads"
    "new construction projects near me"
    "who is building in [city]"

SEGMENT 2: GROWTH-MODE CONTRACTORS (high intent — biggest LTV)
  Doing $500K-$5M, want to scale. They know permits = intel.
  Searches:
    "[city] building permits"
    "new building permits [city] [year]"
    "construction permit data [city]"
    "active construction projects [city]"
    "commercial building permits [city]"
    "[trade] permits [city]" (e.g., "plumbing permits houston")

SEGMENT 3: TRADE-SPECIFIC CONTRACTORS (high intent)
  Roofers, HVAC, plumbers, electricians, solar installers.
  They search by TRADE, not just city.
  Searches:
    "roofing leads [city]"
    "HVAC contractor leads"
    "plumbing permit leads [city]"
    "solar panel permits [city]"
    "electrical permits near me"
    "do I need a permit for [project]"

SEGMENT 4: MARKET RESEARCHERS (medium intent)
  GCs, developers, real estate investors checking market activity.
  Searches:
    "construction activity [city] [year]"
    "building permit trends [year]"
    "fastest growing construction markets"
    "construction boom [city]"
    "permit data by zip code"
    "construction market report [year]"

SEGMENT 5: INFO SEEKERS (top of funnel — nurture to convert)
  Homeowners and new contractors learning the process.
  Searches:
    "how to pull a building permit"
    "building permit cost [city]"
    "how long does a building permit take"
    "do I need a permit for a kitchen remodel"
    "do I need a permit to replace my roof"
    "building permit requirements [city]"


==========================================
THE FULL IMPLEMENTATION — ALL AT ONCE
==========================================

Everything below should be implemented. Code can do it all.
Organized by impact, not difficulty.


--- 1. TRADE-SPECIFIC LANDING PAGES (BIGGEST SEO WIN) ---

Create pages at: /permits/[city]/[trade]
Example: /permits/houston/plumbing

This captures Segment 3 entirely. Nobody in your space has good
pages for "plumbing permits houston" or "roofing leads dallas".

For EACH of your top 30 cities, create pages for these trades:
  - plumbing
  - electrical
  - hvac
  - roofing
  - solar
  - general-construction
  - demolition
  - fire-protection

That's 30 cities x 8 trades = 240 new landing pages.

Each page should include:
  - H1: "[Trade] Building Permits in [City], [ST]"
  - Dynamic permit count for that trade+city combo from DB
  - 5 most recent permits for that trade in that city
  - "About [Trade] Permits in [City]" section (3-4 paragraphs)
  - Local licensing requirements for that trade in that state
  - Average project values for that trade in that city
  - FAQ section (3-5 questions) targeting "People Also Ask":
    * "Do I need a [trade] permit in [City]?"
    * "How much does a [trade] permit cost in [City]?"
    * "How long does a [trade] permit take in [City]?"
  - CTA: "Get [Trade] Leads in [City] — Set Up Free Alerts"
  - Internal links to: city page, state hub, related blog post
  - Schema: Dataset + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList + WebPage

Template variables needed:
  {{ city }}, {{ state }}, {{ trade }}, {{ trade_display }},
  {{ permit_count }}, {{ recent_permits }}, {{ avg_value }},
  {{ total_value }}, {{ faq_items }}

Route: @app.route('/permits/<city_slug>/<trade_slug>')

Add ALL 240 pages to sitemap.xml.

--- 3. PROGRAMMATIC CONTENT ENRICHMENT ---

Google's Helpful Content system penalizes thin programmatic pages.
Your city pages need MORE unique content per city.

3A. Add to EVERY city page template:
  - "Top Neighborhoods" section: Query DB for top 5 zip codes or
    neighborhoods by permit count within that city
  - "Permit Trends" section: Show month-over-month permit count
    (e.g., "Houston saw 1,420 new permits in March 2026, up 12%
    from February")
  - "Top Contractors" section: Show top 3 contractors by permit
    count in that city (links to /contractors filtered by city)
  - "Average Project Value": Calculate from DB for that city
  - "Most Common Project Types": Top 3 permit types in that city

3B. Add to EVERY state hub page:
  - "Construction Market Overview" paragraph (can be templated
    with dynamic stats: total permits, YoY growth, top cities)
  - "Fastest Growing Cities" section: Cities with highest
    month-over-month permit increase
  - "Top Contractors in [State]": Top 5 by permit volume

3C. CITY PAGE FAQ EXPANSION:
  Each city page should have 5-8 FAQs targeting real searches.
  Template-generate from data + city name:
  - "How many active building permits are in [City]?"
    Answer: "As of [month] [year], [City] has [count]+ active..."
  - "What types of construction are most common in [City]?"
    Answer: Based on actual trade distribution data
  - "How do I find new construction projects in [City]?"
    Answer: Links to PermitGrab features
  - "What is the average construction project value in [City]?"
    Answer: Calculated from DB
  - "Which contractors are most active in [City]?"
    Answer: Top 3 from DB data
  These FAQs are DATA-DRIVEN, not generic — Google rewards that.

--- 6. TECHNICAL SEO IMPROVEMENTS ---

6A. PAGE SPEED:
  - Add <link rel="preconnect"> for any external resources (fonts, CDN)
  - Add <link rel="preload"> for critical CSS/JS
  - Verify images use WebP format with proper dimensions
  - Add loading="lazy" to below-fold images
  - Consider implementing next-gen caching headers

6B. SITEMAP ENHANCEMENTS:
  - Add <lastmod> dates to all sitemap URLs (use last data update)
  - Add <changefreq> hints:
    * City/trade pages: "daily" (data updates daily)
    * Blog posts: "monthly"
    * Static pages: "monthly"
  - Add <priority> hints:
    * Homepage: 1.0
    * Top city pages: 0.9
    * Trade pages: 0.8
    * State hubs: 0.8
    * Blog posts: 0.7
    * Static pages: 0.5
  - Include ALL new trade pages in sitemap
  - Create a blog-specific sitemap (sitemap-blog.xml) and
    main sitemap index pointing to both

6C. URL STRUCTURE:
  Keep current clean structure. Add trade pages as:
  /permits/houston/plumbing (not /permits/houston?trade=plumbing)
  Clean URLs rank better than query parameters.

6D. MOBILE OPTIMIZATION:
  - Verify Core Web Vitals pass on mobile (LCP < 2.5s, FID < 100ms,
    CLS < 0.1)
  - Test all new trade pages render correctly on 375px width
  - Ensure tap targets are 48px+ for mobile users

6E. INDEXING:
  - After deploying all new pages, submit updated sitemap to
    Google Search Console
  - Use Google's URL Inspection tool to request indexing of
    high-priority new trade pages
  - Monitor Index Coverage report for any errors


--- 7. AI SEARCH OPTIMIZATION (GEO) ---

Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity are increasingly
answering contractor queries. Structured data is how you get cited.

7A. Make data machine-readable:
  - Every data point (permit counts, values, trends) should be
    in schema markup, not just visible text
  - Use Dataset schema liberally — your data IS your moat

7B. Write content that AI can quote:
  - Include clear, factual statements AI can extract:
    "Houston has 4,357 active building permits as of March 2026"
    "The average commercial permit value in Houston is $2.1M"
  - Structure with clear Q&A format in FAQs

7C. Entity establishment:
  - Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) if applicable
  - Same organization schema on every page
  - Build mentions/citations on construction industry sites
