## Fort Lauderdale's Building Permit System

Fort Lauderdale manages building permits through the **Development Services Department**, which oversees the city's comprehensive permitting process. The city uses a modern online permitting system that allows contractors and property owners to submit applications, track review status, and schedule inspections electronically.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** Fort Lauderdale's permitting portal allows you to submit applications, upload plans, check status, and schedule inspections 24/7. The online system is the fastest path to permit approval and provides real-time updates on your application.

**In-Person:** Visit the Development Services Department at **100 N. Andrews Avenue, Fort Lauderdale, FL 33301**. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM. In-person visits may be necessary for complex waterfront or high-rise projects that require pre-application meetings.

**Phone Support:** (954) 828-5921 for permits and inspections (Monday-Friday during business hours)

### Permit Costs in Fort Lauderdale

Fort Lauderdale's fee structure reflects the city's status as a premium development market:

- **Residential permits (minor work):** $200-$900
- **Residential renovation/addition permits:** $1,500-$6,000
- **Commercial building permits:** $4,000-$20,000+ based on project valuation
- **High-rise residential permits:** $10,000-$50,000+ for luxury condominiums
- **Waterfront/marine permits:** Additional environmental review fees
- **Electrical, plumbing, mechanical permits:** $200-$800 each
- **Fence permits:** $100-$400
- **Demolition permits:** $400-$1,500

**Key advantage:** Downtown development projects may qualify for expedited review and incentive programs as part of urban revitalization initiatives along Las Olas Boulevard and the central downtown core.

### Fort Lauderdale's Permit Timeline

Fort Lauderdale's permitting process follows a structured workflow, with additional time for waterfront and high-rise projects:

1. **Submission** — Same-day online or next business day in-person
2. **Pre-screening** — 1-3 business days for completeness check
3. **Plan review** — 10-20 business days residential, 20-35 commercial/waterfront
4. **Environmental review** — Waterfront and coastal projects require additional environmental clearance
5. **Revision cycles** — If corrections needed, 5-10 business days per additional cycle
6. **Fee collection and issuance** — Upon approval, permits are issued after fees are paid
7. **Inspections** — Scheduled throughout the construction period

Projects with no plan review required (fences, minor repairs) can be issued same-day in many cases. Waterfront and high-rise projects typically require longer review periods due to coastal zone management, structural engineering, and environmental considerations.
