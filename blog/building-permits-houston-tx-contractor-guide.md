## Houston's Building Permit System

Houston processes permits through the **City of Houston Development Services Department** (DSD), one of the most active permit offices in Texas.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** The City of Houston uses an online permit portal at **houstontx.gov/development-services**. This is the fastest way to submit residential and commercial permits. You can track your application status in real-time and receive automated updates.

**In-Person:** Visit the Development Services office at **1002 Washington Ave, Houston, TX 77002** (Downtown). Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 4:30 PM. Expect wait times of 30-60 minutes during peak hours.

**Phone:** (713) 837-0000 for general inquiries (Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM)

### Permit Types and Costs

Houston breaks permits into several categories:

- **Residential building permits:** $1,500-$4,000 for additions, remodels, and renovations
- **Commercial building permits:** $3,000-$10,000+ depending on square footage and construction type
- **Plan review fees:** $500-$3,000 depending on complexity
- **Electrical, mechanical, plumbing permits:** $300-$1,000 each (often required as separate permits)

The city provides an [online fee calculator](https://houstontx.gov/development-services/permit-fees) to estimate costs based on project valuation.

### Timeline Expectations

Houston's review process typically follows this timeline:

1. **Submission** — Same day online, next day in-person
2. **Initial review** — 15-30 business days for most residential permits
3. **Plan corrections** — If required, add 5-10 business days per resubmittal
4. **Permit issuance** — Once all corrections are approved

Fast-track review is available for an additional 50% fee if you need permits quickly.

## How to Use Houston Building Permits to Find Leads

Successful contractors don't wait for leads to come to them — they use permit data to find prospects before the competition does.

**Monitor permit applications.** When a homeowner or developer pulls a permit, they've already committed to a project. That's your signal to reach out with complementary services or bid on related work.

**Track permit trends by neighborhood.** Some Houston neighborhoods are heating up faster than others. By analyzing permit activity, you can position yourself in high-growth areas before they become saturated.

**Identify general contractors pulling large projects.** If you're a subcontractor, watch for GCs pulling permits on commercial or multifamily jobs in your trade. Reach out with a proposal or capability statement.

**Find developers and property owners.** Permit records include owner and contractor information. Build a prospect list of developers actively building in Houston.

**Spot seasonal patterns.** Houston construction activity peaks in spring and fall. Using historical permit data helps you forecast demand and plan staffing.

## Final Tips for Houston Contractors

**Know the timeline.** Houston's permit review takes 2-4 weeks for standard residential projects. Factor this into your project planning and customer communications.

**Budget for MEP permits.** Most projects require separate electrical, mechanical, and plumbing permits. This adds $1,000-$3,000 to costs and extends timelines.

**Check for deed restrictions.** Houston has many deed-restricted subdivisions with covenants beyond city code. Always verify restrictions before designing or bidding.

**Plan for inspections.** Houston requires final inspections for most permits. Schedule early to avoid project delays.
