## Honolulu's Building Permit System

Honolulu's building permits are managed by the **Department of Planning and Permitting (DPP)**, which oversees construction across the City and County.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** The **DPP ePermit system** offers 24/7 access to submit permits, track status, upload documents, and pay fees. This is the fastest and most efficient route for most projects.

**In-Person:** Visit the **Frank F. Fasi Municipal Building, 650 South King Street, Honolulu, HI 96813**. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 4 PM. Professional staff can answer questions and assist with submissions.

**Phone Support:** (808) 768-8000 for permit inquiries and general guidance (Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 4 PM)

### Permit Costs in Honolulu

Honolulu's permit fees are calculated based on estimated value of work and project type:

- **Minor residential work:** $200-$500
- **Residential additions and renovations:** $500-$2,000
- **ADU and Ohana units:** $800-$3,000
- **Solar installations:** $400-$1,200
- **New construction:** $2,000-$10,000+
- **Commercial projects:** $3,000-$15,000+

Island logistics add cost and complexity compared to mainland markets. Material shipping, limited contractor availability, and higher labor costs impact project budgets.

### Honolulu's Permit Timeline

Timeline varies significantly by project type and complexity:

1. **Simple residential permits** — 5-15 business days
2. **Residential additions** — 20-40 business days
3. **New construction** — 30-60+ business days
4. **SMA (Coastal) permits** — Additional 15-30 days for environmental review
5. **Solar and EV permits** — 10-20 business days (fast-track available)

Submission completeness and flood hazard zone documentation affect timeline. ePermit electronic submission generally accelerates review compared to in-person filing.
