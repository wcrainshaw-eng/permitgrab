## Understanding San Diego County's Permit System

San Diego County's permitting system is managed by the **Department of Planning & Development Services (PDS)** with offices throughout the county. The system uses the **Socrata platform**, which integrates contractor information directly into permit records — a significant advantage for lead generation.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** Submit all permits through **sdcounty.ca.gov/pds**. The online portal is comprehensive, allowing full permit submission, real-time status tracking, and document uploads. This is the standard method — 95% of contractors use it exclusively.

**In-Person:** Regional offices are located in:
- **San Diego** (Downtown) — 1600 Pacific Hwy, San Diego, CA 92101
- **Escondido** — 1888 California Ave, Escondido, CA 92025
- **Ramona** — 1235 Main St, Ramona, CA 92065
- **Julian** — 1225 Main St, Julian, CA 92036

Hours: 8:30 AM-4:30 PM, Monday-Friday (walk-ins welcome)

**Phone Support:** (858) 694-2960 for general permit questions

### Permit Costs in San Diego County

San Diego County's permit structure rewards early planning:

- **Residential remodel permits:** $1,200-$3,000
- **Residential addition permits:** $1,500-$4,000
- **Commercial permits:** $5,000-$20,000+ based on valuation
- **Electrical/plumbing/HVAC trade permits:** $400-$1,200 each
- **Plan review fees:** $500-$3,000
- **Expedited review surcharge:** 20-30% additional fee for 5-7 day turnaround

The [County PDS fee calculator](https://sdcounty.ca.gov/pds) provides exact estimates based on construction value.

### Timeline & Expedited Options

San Diego's timeline varies by permit complexity:

1. **Submission** — Same day online
2. **Initial review** — 10-15 business days (standard)
3. **Plan corrections** — 3-5 business days per revision round
4. **Final approval** — Upon completion of review

**Expedited review** (optional) reduces initial review to 5-7 days but costs 20-30% more in fees. For projects with tight deadlines, expedited review is often worth the investment.

**South County Development:** Chula Vista and Imperial Beach are experiencing population growth with associated residential construction and renovations.
