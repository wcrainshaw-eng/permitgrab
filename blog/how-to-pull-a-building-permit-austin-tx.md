If you're a contractor working in Austin, you know the construction market here is on fire. From new builds in Mueller to renovation projects in Hyde Park, there's no shortage of work — but every job starts with the right permits. Here's everything you need to know about pulling a building permit in Austin.

## Where to Apply

Austin handles building permits through **Austin Build + Connect (AB+C)**, part of the city's Development Services Department. You can apply two ways:

**Online:** Visit [austintexas.gov/abc-applications](https://www.austintexas.gov/abc-applications) and use the application wizard to walk through the submission process. This is the fastest option for most residential projects.

**In-Person:** Visit the Permitting and Development Center at **6310 Wilhelmina Delco Dr., Austin, TX 78752**. Walk-in hours are Monday through Friday, 7:30 AM to 3:30 PM.

For questions, call **(512) 978-4000** (Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM).

## What Does an Austin Building Permit Cost?

Permit fees in Austin vary by project scope:

- **Application processing fee:** Under $100 for most residential projects
- **Plan review fees:** Typically $1,000+ depending on project complexity
- **Mechanical/electrical/plumbing:** Additional permits required with separate fees

For a standard residential addition or remodel, expect total permit costs between $1,000 and $3,000. New construction will run higher based on project valuation.

## How Long Does It Take?

Plan on **6 to 12 weeks** from application to permit issuance for most residential projects. Here's the typical timeline:

1. **Submit application** — same day online or in-person
2. **Initial plan review** — 15 to 25 business days
3. **Corrections/resubmittal** — if needed, add 1-2 weeks per round
4. **Permit issuance** — once plans are approved

Simple projects like re-roofing or HVAC replacements may qualify for express or over-the-counter permits, which can be issued the same day.

## What You'll Need

Before applying, gather these documents:

- Completed application form
- Construction plans and specifications (to scale)
- Site plan showing the property and proposed work
- Proof of contractor license or homeowner authorization
- Energy compliance documentation (IECC compliance)
- Tree survey (if applicable — Austin takes its trees seriously)

## Tips From Austin Contractors

**Start early.** Austin's review times have been stretching as the city grows. Don't wait until the last minute.

**Use the online portal.** In-person visits mean waiting in line. The online wizard catches missing info before you submit.

**Check for deed restrictions.** Many Austin neighborhoods have HOA or deed restrictions that go beyond city code. Verify before you design.

**Budget for multiple permits.** A renovation often needs separate building, electrical, mechanical, and plumbing permits. Factor all of them into your timeline.
