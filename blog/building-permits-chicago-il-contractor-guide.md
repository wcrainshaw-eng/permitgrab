## Chicago's Building Permit System

Chicago handles building permits through the **Department of Buildings (DOB)**, which manages one of the nation's most active permitting systems.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** Chicago's **E-Plan Review** system and DOB online portal offer 24/7 access to submit permits, track status, and upload documents. This is the fastest route.

**In-Person:** Visit City Hall at **121 N LaSalle St, Room 906, Chicago, IL 60602**. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 4 PM. Walk-in service is available.

**Phone Support:** (312) 744-3449 for permit inquiries and assistance (Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 4 PM)

### Permit Costs in Chicago

Chicago's permit fees vary widely by project scope and are structured by building type and work category:

- **Minor residential permits:** $100-$500
- **Residential renovations:** $500-$3,000
- **Residential additions:** $1,500-$5,000
- **Commercial permits:** $5,000-$25,000+
- **Fee breakdown:** Building fees, zoning fees, and other charges itemized separately

The Department of Buildings publishes its complete fee schedule online, with options to estimate costs based on project valuation.

### Chicago's Permit Timeline

Chicago offers accelerated options for qualifying projects:

1. **Express Permit Program** — 1-3 business days for qualifying projects
2. **Easy Permit Process** — Fast-tracked review for smaller residential work
3. **Standard residential** — 15-30 business days
4. **Commercial projects** — 20-45 business days
5. **E-Plan review** — Available for faster digital processing

Timeline depends on project complexity and submission completeness.
