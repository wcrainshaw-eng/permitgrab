## Orlando's Building Permit System

Orlando manages building permits through the **Permitting Services Division**, which operates under the City's Planning and Development department. The city uses a modern online permitting system that allows contractors and property owners to submit applications, track review status, and schedule inspections electronically.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** Orlando's permitting portal allows you to submit applications, upload plans, check status, and schedule inspections 24/7. The online system is the fastest path to permit approval and provides real-time updates on your application.

**In-Person:** Visit the Permitting Services Division at **Orlando City Hall, 400 S. Orange Avenue, Orlando, FL 32801**. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM. In-person visits may be necessary for complex projects that require pre-application meetings.

**Phone Support:** (407) 246-2271 for permits and inspections (Monday-Friday during business hours)

### Permit Costs in Orlando

Orlando's fee structure is competitive with other Florida metro areas:

- **Residential permits (minor work):** $200-$800
- **Residential renovation/addition permits:** $1,000-$5,000
- **Commercial building permits:** $3,000-$15,000+ based on project valuation
- **Electrical, plumbing, mechanical permits:** $150-$600 each
- **Fence permits:** $100-$300
- **Demolition permits:** $300-$1,200

**Key advantage:** Projects in Community Redevelopment Areas (CRAs) like Parramore, the Milk District, and portions of Colonial Drive may qualify for incentive programs including fee reductions and expedited review.

### Orlando's Permit Timeline

Orlando's permitting process follows a structured workflow:

1. **Submission** — Same-day online or next business day in-person
2. **Pre-screening** — 1-3 business days for completeness check
3. **Plan review** — 10-20 business days residential, 15-30 commercial
4. **Revision cycles** — If corrections needed, 5-10 business days per additional cycle
5. **Fee collection and issuance** — Upon approval, permits are issued after fees are paid
6. **Inspections** — Scheduled throughout the construction period

Projects with no plan review required (fences, minor repairs) can be issued same-day in many cases.

## Orlando's Construction Boom: Where the Growth Is

Orlando's construction activity spans diverse sectors and neighborhoods, each with distinct opportunities for contractors:

**Lake Nona:** The southeast Orlando master-planned community continues its explosive growth trajectory. Medical City at Lake Nona houses the VA Medical Center, Nemours Children's Hospital, and UCF College of Medicine, with ongoing hospital and research facility expansions driving healthcare construction. Thousands of new residential units are under development alongside commercial and retail centers.

**Thornton Park & Downtown:** The urban core is experiencing a residential renaissance. Historic buildings are being converted to lofts and mixed-use spaces. New apartment towers and condominiums are rising along Orange Avenue and around Lake Eola. Downtown's walkable districts attract luxury residential and high-end restaurant/retail construction.

**Milk District:** This emerging neighborhood east of downtown has become Orlando's arts and culture hub. Historic commercial buildings are being renovated into breweries, galleries, restaurants, and creative offices. Residential infill and renovation activity is accelerating as the neighborhood gentrifies.

**International Drive Corridor:** The tourism backbone of Central Florida continues to evolve with new hotel construction, entertainment venue development, and the ongoing transformation associated with the I-Drive area. Convention center expansion and related hospitality construction provide steady work for commercial contractors.

**SoDo (South of Downtown):** The area south of downtown along Orange Avenue is experiencing rapid commercial and residential development. Warehouse and light industrial buildings are being converted to office, retail, and restaurant spaces. New mixed-use projects are changing the neighborhood's character.

**Creative Village:** The former Amway Arena site is being developed into a mixed-use innovation district. UCF Downtown campus anchors the development, which includes student housing, market-rate apartments, office space, and retail. Construction activity here spans years of phased development.

## Pro Tips for Orlando Contractors

**Understand Florida licensing requirements.** Florida's contractor licensing is administered by the DBPR. Make sure your state certification or registration is current before bidding work. Orlando strictly enforces licensing requirements.

**Learn CRA incentive zones.** Projects in Community Redevelopment Areas may qualify for reduced fees, tax incentives, or expedited review. Understanding these zones makes your bids more competitive and your projects more profitable.

**Plan for Florida's building code.** Florida has some of the nation's strictest building codes, especially for wind resistance and energy efficiency. Familiarity with Florida Building Code requirements speeds permit approvals and reduces revision cycles.

**Account for hurricane season.** June through November is hurricane season, which can impact exterior construction schedules. Interior renovation work tends to increase during these months. Plan staffing and project timelines accordingly.

**Build developer relationships in growth areas.** Lake Nona, Creative Village, and the I-Drive corridor have institutional developers with multiple ongoing projects. Permit data reveals which developers are most active, letting you target relationship-building efforts.

**Monitor tourism infrastructure spending.** Orlando's tourism economy drives billions in construction investment annually. Hotels, restaurants, entertainment venues, and convention facilities create steady demand for commercial construction services year-round.
