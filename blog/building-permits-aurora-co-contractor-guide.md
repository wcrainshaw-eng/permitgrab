Aurora, Colorado is one of the fastest-growing cities in the Denver metropolitan area and the third-largest city in Colorado. The city's construction market is undergoing rapid transformation, driven by major developments at the Fitzsimons/Anschutz Medical Campus, residential expansion near Denver International Airport, commercial growth along major corridors, and downtown revitalization efforts. This creates exceptional opportunity for contractors who understand Aurora's permit system and where development is concentrated.

For contractors, Aurora represents a diverse market spanning medical facility construction, suburban residential development, commercial projects, and infrastructure work. Success here requires understanding Aurora's Planning and Development Services, where permits are tracked, which neighborhoods are generating the most opportunities, and how to position for work in high-growth corridors like the E-470 corridor, Southlands, and the DIA region.

## Aurora's Building Permit System

Aurora manages building permits through **Planning and Development Services**, which operates from the Aurora Municipal Center. The city has been rapidly expanding its permit processing capacity to handle growth, while maintaining code compliance and inspection standards.

### Where to Apply

**In-Person:** Visit **Aurora Municipal Center, 15151 E Alameda Parkway, Aurora, CO 80012**. Planning and Development Services is housed here along with other city departments. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 4:30 PM. In-person submission is currently the primary method for new permit applications.

**Phone Support:** (303) 739-7420 for permit questions, application status, and inspection scheduling.

**Online Options:** Aurora is expanding online permit submission capabilities. Contact Planning and Development Services to inquire about current online options and ePlan availability.

### Permit Costs in Aurora

Aurora's fee structure reflects the city's diverse construction market:

- **Residential minor permits (fences, minor alterations):** $200-$400
- **Standard residential permits:** $300-$600 based on valuation
- **Roofing permits:** $150-$400
- **Multi-family residential permits:** $400-$1,500+ based on unit count and valuation
- **Commercial permits:** $1,500-$10,000+ based on project valuation
- **Electrical, plumbing, mechanical permits:** $150-$500 each
- **Demolition permits:** $300-$800

**Key advantage:** Aurora's permit data includes permit type, project description, property address, application date, issue date, permit status, and valuation—providing valuable insight into construction activity across the metro area.

### Aurora's Permit Timeline

Aurora's permitting follows a structured process designed to handle growing demand:

1. **Submission** — In-person at Aurora Municipal Center or through expanding online options
2. **Plan review** — 10-15 days for standard residential, 20-30 for complex projects and commercial
3. **Revision cycles** — 5-10 business days per additional review if corrections needed
4. **Permit issuance** — Upon approval and fee payment
5. **Inspections** — Scheduled during construction through Planning and Development Services

Aurora's growth means permit processing times can fluctuate. Contacting the city directly provides the most current timeline expectations.

## Aurora's Construction Market: Where the Growth Is

Aurora's construction activity is concentrated in distinct areas, each with unique characteristics and growth potential:

**Fitzsimons / Anschutz Medical Campus:** The epicenter of Aurora's commercial and institutional construction. The University of Colorado's Anschutz Medical Campus is continuously expanding with new research facilities, clinical buildings, educational structures, and ancillary commercial development. This area attracts specialized contractors experienced with healthcare and research facility construction. Project valuations here are consistently high, and work includes MEP, structural, laboratory-specific trades, and commercial buildout.

**Southlands / South Aurora:** One of Aurora's major residential growth areas, with ongoing development of master-planned communities, single-family neighborhoods, and mixed-use projects. This area is generating consistent residential construction permits and representing significant new home construction opportunity. Infrastructure development and landscape work accompany residential buildout.

**DIA Corridor / South Aurora Residential:** The area surrounding Denver International Airport is experiencing explosive residential growth as the region develops. New home construction, multi-family projects, and supporting commercial development drive permit activity. This corridor represents some of the strongest residential construction activity in the metro area.

**E-470 Corridor:** Commercial and light industrial development along this major transportation corridor is significant. Office, warehouse, logistics, and commercial retail construction make this area valuable for commercial contractors. The corridor's accessibility makes it attractive for distribution and business park development.

**Original Aurora / Colfax Revitalization:** Downtown Aurora is experiencing revitalization with mixed-use development, residential conversions, local retail, and restaurant construction. This historic area represents opportunities for adaptive reuse, interior renovation, and commercial build-outs. Historic preservation considerations may apply to some projects.

**Stapleton / Central Park Adjacent:** The former Stapleton Airport site redevelopment and adjacent Central Park neighborhood continue to mature, generating residential, retail, and community facility construction. This area represents mid-to-long-term development opportunity with ongoing infrastructure and building projects.
