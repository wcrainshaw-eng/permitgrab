## Atlanta's Building Permit System

Atlanta handles building permits through the **Department of Buildings**, part of the City of Atlanta. The department processes both residential and commercial permits and has modernized its system to handle the volume.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** The City of Atlanta's online permit portal is available at **atlanta.gov/buildings**. This is the fastest method for submitting residential and commercial permits. You can track your application 24/7 and upload documents as needed.

**In-Person:** Visit the Department of Buildings at **55 Trinity Ave SW, Suite 3200, Atlanta, GA 30303** (Downtown). Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 4:30 PM. Walk-ins are welcome but expect wait times during peak hours.

**Phone Support:** (404) 330-6110 for permit questions and assistance

### Permit Costs in Atlanta

Atlanta's permit fees are reasonable compared to other major Southeast metros:

- **Residential remodel permits:** $1,000-$2,500
- **Residential addition permits:** $1,500-$3,500
- **Commercial permits:** $3,000-$25,000+ based on construction cost
- **Plan review fees:** $300-$2,000
- **MEP permits:** $200-$800 each (electrical, mechanical, plumbing)

The city's online portal includes a fee calculator to estimate costs for your specific project.

### Atlanta's Permit Timeline

Atlanta's recent upgrades to its permitting system have significantly improved review times:

1. **Submission** — Same day online
2. **Initial review** — 10-20 business days for residential, 15-30 for commercial
3. **Plan corrections** — If needed, 5-10 business days per round
4. **Permit issuance** — Upon final approval

Simple projects like roofing or HVAC replacements may qualify for same-day or next-day permits.

## Atlanta's Construction Boom: Where Growth Is Happening

Atlanta's growth is concentrated in several key areas, creating distinct opportunities for contractors:

**Downtown & Midtown:** Office towers, residential high-rises, and mixed-use projects dominate downtown. Midtown is the epicenter of Atlanta's tech and office boom. Billions in investment are flowing into the urban core.

**Buckhead:** This affluent neighborhood is experiencing major commercial and residential development. New office buildings, upscale residences, and retail projects are reshaping the district.

**East Atlanta & Old Fourth Ward:** Urban neighborhoods are undergoing rapid gentrification with residential renovations, new construction, and mixed-use development. This is one of Atlanta's hottest real estate markets.

**Beltline District:** The BeltLine Trail project has catalyzed development along the historic rail corridor. New parks, residential projects, and retail are transforming the area.

**Suburban Explosion:** Northern suburbs (Alpharetta, Johns Creek, Marietta) and southern suburbs (Peachtree City, Fayetteville) are experiencing explosive residential growth. Multifamily housing developments are the primary driver.

**Airport District & Logistics:** Hartsfield-Jackson's continued importance as a major aviation hub drives logistics and industrial construction in surrounding areas.
