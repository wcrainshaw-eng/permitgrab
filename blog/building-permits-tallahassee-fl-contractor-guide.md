## Tallahassee's Building Permit System

Tallahassee manages building permits through a joint system administered by **Leon County Growth Management** and the **City of Tallahassee Planning Department**. Properties within the city limits use the city system, while county properties use the Leon County system. Both operate modern permitting platforms that allow contractors and property owners to submit applications, track review status, and schedule inspections electronically.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** Leon County's growth management system and the City of Tallahassee's permitting portal both allow online submission 24/7. Online applications provide the fastest path to permit approval and real-time status tracking. This is the preferred method for most contractors.

**In-Person:**
- **Leon County:** Leon County Growth Management, 2280 Centre Pointe Boulevard, Tallahassee, FL 32308
- **City of Tallahassee:** Planning Department, 300 S. Adams Street, Tallahassee, FL 32301. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM. In-person visits may be necessary for complex projects requiring pre-application meetings.

**Phone Support:** (850) 891-8600 for both Leon County and City of Tallahassee (Monday-Friday during business hours)

### Permit Costs in Tallahassee

Tallahassee's fee structure is competitive with other Florida markets:

- **Residential permits (minor work):** $200-$600
- **Residential renovation/addition permits:** $800-$3,500
- **Commercial building permits:** $2,500-$12,000+ based on project valuation
- **Electrical, plumbing, mechanical permits:** $125-$500 each
- **Fence permits:** $75-$250
- **Demolition permits:** $250-$1,000

**Key advantage:** Some projects in designated growth areas may qualify for incentive programs including fee reductions and expedited processing.

### Tallahassee's Permit Timeline

Tallahassee's permitting process follows a structured workflow:

1. **Submission** — Same-day online or next business day in-person
2. **Pre-screening** — 1-3 business days for completeness check
3. **Plan review** — 10-20 business days residential, 15-30 commercial
4. **Revision cycles** — If corrections needed, 5-10 business days per additional cycle
5. **Fee collection and issuance** — Upon approval, permits are issued after fees are paid
6. **Inspections** — Scheduled throughout the construction period

Projects with no plan review required (fences, minor repairs) can be issued same-day in many cases.

## Tallahassee's Construction Market: Where the Growth Is

Tallahassee's construction activity spans diverse sectors and neighborhoods, each with distinct opportunities for contractors:

**Downtown & Midtown:** Tallahassee's urban core is experiencing downtown revitalization along with growing Midtown development. Historic commercial buildings are being converted to lofts, creative offices, restaurants, and retail spaces. The Midtown corridor attracts boutique hotels, mixed-use projects, and specialty retail that drive renovation and new construction activity.

**CollegeTown (FSU Area):** Florida State University's continued growth drives major construction in the north campus area and surrounding neighborhoods. Student housing projects, academic buildings, and related retail/dining development are constant. FAMU's nearby campus also generates education and housing construction. The university corridor is one of Tallahassee's hottest construction zones.

**Southwood:** This master-planned community on the south side continues residential expansion with single-family homes, townhomes, and mixed-use village centers. Planned growth here generates consistent residential construction, site development, and commercial infill work.

**Killearn Estates & NE Growth Corridors:** Established residential neighborhoods and new growth areas in northeast Tallahassee support steady residential construction, renovation activity, and commercial development. Rising population in these corridors drives infrastructure and retail development.

**Capital Circle Corridors:** The business corridors ringing the city (particularly north, south, and east Capital Circle) attract office, retail, and mixed-use development. Corporate campuses, medical offices, and professional services buildings generate commercial construction opportunities.

**Government Campus:** State government facility construction and renovation remains a constant source of work. Legislative, agency, and administrative building projects provide large-scale commercial opportunities for general contractors and specialty trades.

## Pro Tips for Tallahassee Contractors

**Understand Florida licensing requirements.** Florida's contractor licensing is administered by the DBPR. Make sure your state certification or registration is current before bidding work. Tallahassee and Leon County strictly enforce licensing requirements.

**Know the government contracting process.** Many state government projects follow specific procurement procedures. Understanding how to bid government work and building relationships with government agencies can unlock significant contracts. University projects may also have specific requirements.

**Plan for Florida's building code.** Florida has some of the nation's strictest building codes, especially for wind resistance and energy efficiency. Familiarity with Florida Building Code requirements speeds permit approvals and reduces revision cycles.

**Build university relationships.** FSU and FAMU have ongoing construction programs. Permit data reveals which contractors are winning university work. Consider building relationships with university procurement departments and general contractors winning bids.

**Account for hurricane season.** June through November is hurricane season, which can impact exterior construction schedules. Interior renovation work tends to increase during these months. Plan staffing and project timelines accordingly.

**Monitor downtown revitalization spending.** Tallahassee's Midtown and downtown revitalization initiatives drive billions in investment. Following these projects reveals development trends and identifies active developers and contractors.

**Track state government capital spending.** Legislative sessions and budget priorities drive government construction cycles. Understanding the state budget calendar helps you anticipate government building projects and renovation activity.
