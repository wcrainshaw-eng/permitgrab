# How Much Does a Building Permit Cost in Chicago? (2026 Fee Guide)

Chicago's building permit fees are calculated using a formula based on construction type, occupancy classification, and project scope — not a flat rate. That means your permit cost can vary significantly depending on what you're building and where. This guide breaks down what to expect so you can budget accurately before starting a project.

## Chicago's Permit Fee Structure

Unlike cities that charge a simple flat fee or a percentage of construction cost, Chicago uses a formula defined in the Chicago Building Code. The calculation factors in four variables:

- **Construction type** (wood frame, steel, concrete, etc.)
- **Occupancy type** (residential, commercial, assembly, etc.)
- **Area** in square feet
- **Project scope** (new construction, alteration, repair)

The City of Chicago provides an online fee calculator at the Department of Buildings website (chicago.gov) where you can plug in your project details and get an estimate. The calculator was built on fee tables that are updated periodically — the most recent tables took effect January 1, 2025.

**The minimum permit fee in Chicago is $302** for any permit that requires plan review and drawings. Even a small residential alteration won't cost less than this. This minimum applies to the building permit itself — electrical, plumbing, and mechanical permits are separate and have their own fees.

## What Drives the Cost Up

**Square footage is the primary driver.** Chicago's fee tables are structured around the area of work. A 200-square-foot bathroom remodel generates a lower fee than a 2,000-square-foot addition, even if both are residential alterations.

**Occupancy type changes the multiplier.** Commercial and mixed-use buildings face higher per-square-foot rates than single-family residential because they require more intensive plan review. A restaurant build-out is more expensive to permit than a residential renovation of equivalent size due to additional fire safety, accessibility, and occupancy load requirements.

**Multiple trades stack up.** A kitchen remodel typically requires separate permits for building, electrical, plumbing, and sometimes mechanical work. Each permit has its own fee. When budgeting for a client, account for all the permits the project will need, not just the building permit.

**Zoning reviews add cost.** If your project doesn't comply with current zoning — for example, you're building closer to the property line than the setback allows, or changing the use of a space — you may need a zoning variance or special use permit. These involve hearings and additional fees that can add significantly to the total cost and timeline.

**Pre-1985 buildings require asbestos surveys.** For any demolition or renovation work in buildings constructed before 1985, Chicago requires an asbestos survey before issuing the permit. This isn't a city fee — it's a separate cost paid to an environmental testing firm — but the permit won't be issued without it. Budget accordingly.

## Permit Processing Timelines

How long it takes to get your permit depends on the review track your project falls into:

**Easy Permit (formerly Express Permit):** For straightforward work that meets specific criteria. These can be issued quickly, sometimes same-day or within a few business days. Examples include basic electrical or plumbing work, re-roofing, and simple residential repairs.

**Standard Plan Review:** For projects that require full plan review by the Department of Buildings. Expect several weeks for residential projects, longer for commercial. The timeline depends on current workload and the complexity of your submittal.

**Developer Services:** For larger projects. Timeline varies based on scope.

The clock starts when you submit a **complete** application. Incomplete submittals get returned without review, which can waste weeks. Double-check that all required documents, drawings, and supporting materials are included before submitting.

## Tips for Chicago Contractors

**Use the online fee calculator before quoting jobs.** Permit costs should be part of your estimate, not an unpleasant surprise for the client. Run the numbers through the city's calculator before you bid.

**Keep your E-Plan account current.** Chicago's building permit applications go through their online permitting system. Having your account set up, your documents formatted to their specifications, and your application complete before submitting will avoid back-and-forth that delays the process.

**Factor in all permits, not just the building permit.** A residential renovation might require building, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical permits. Total permit cost is the sum of all of them, and they may need to be filed separately.

**Consider hiring a permit expediter for complex projects.** Many Chicago contractors use professional expediters who know the Department of Buildings' processes and can help navigate the system more efficiently, particularly for projects that involve zoning review or multiple departments.

**Check for fee exemptions.** Some types of work may qualify for reduced fees or exemptions. Check the current fee tables and consult with the Department of Buildings if you think your project might qualify.
