## Sacramento's Building Permit System

Sacramento manages building permits through the **City of Sacramento Development Services Department**, which operates a modern Accela-based permitting system that is considered one of California's most efficient.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** Sacramento's Accela-based system is accessible at **permits.cityofsacramento.org**. This platform handles all permit types, including ADUs, residential, and commercial applications. It's available 24/7 and allows document uploads, real-time status tracking, and inspection scheduling.

**In-Person:** Visit the Development Services Building at **300 Richards Blvd, Sacramento, CA 95811, 1st Floor**. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM. In-person submission is slower but sometimes necessary for complex applications.

**Phone Support:** (916) 808-7200 for permits, inspections, and general questions (Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM)

### Permit Costs in Sacramento

Sacramento's fees are competitive compared to other California cities:

- **Residential permits:** $800-$2,000
- **ADU permits:** $1,500-$4,000 (streamlined pathway available)
- **Residential addition/remodel:** $1,200-$3,000
- **Commercial permits:** $3,000-$15,000+ based on construction value
- **Electrical, plumbing, mechanical permits:** $300-$900 each
- **Title 24 energy compliance review:** Included in permit fee

**Key advantage:** Sacramento participates in California's ADU streamlining program, which can reduce permit fees and review times for qualifying ADU projects.

### Sacramento's Permit Timeline

Sacramento has significantly improved permit processing:

1. **Submission** — Same day online or next business day in-person
2. **Initial completeness review** — 3-5 business days
3. **Substantive review** — 10-25 business days for residential, 15-35 for commercial
4. **Title 24 energy review** — 5-10 business days (part of overall timeline)
5. **Plan corrections** — If required, 5-10 days per round
6. **Permit issuance** — Upon final approval

ADU applications often process in 10-20 days total due to state streamlining requirements.
