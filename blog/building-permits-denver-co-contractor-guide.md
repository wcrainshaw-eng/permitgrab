## Denver's Building Permit System

Denver handles building permits through the **Department of Community Planning and Development (DCPD)**, which operates one of the most modern permitting systems in the country.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** Denver's integrated permitting system, **IPRP** (Integrated Plan Review and Permitting System), is available at **denvergov.org/pocketgov**. This is the fastest way to submit permits, track status, and upload documents. The system is user-friendly and accessible 24/7.

**In-Person:** Visit the Development Services Building (DSD) at **201 W. Colfax Ave, 4th Floor, Denver, CO 80202**. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 4:30 PM. Walk-ins are accepted but expect longer wait times than online submission.

**Phone Support:** (720) 913-1311 for permit inquiries and assistance (Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM)

### Permit Costs in Denver

Denver's permit fees are percentage-based on construction cost and fairly predictable:

- **Residential remodel permits:** $1,500-$2,500
- **Residential addition permits:** $2,000-$4,000
- **Commercial permits:** $3,000-$20,000+ depending on project valuation
- **Plan review fees:** $200-$1,500
- **Trade-specific permits:** $300-$1,200 (electrical, mechanical, plumbing)

The city's [online fee estimator](https://denvergov.org/pocketgov/applications/DPD/DPDFeeCalc.aspx) calculates exact fees based on your project's estimated cost.

### Denver's Permit Timeline

Denver publishes monthly average review times, which have improved significantly in recent years:

1. **Submission** — Same day online or next business day in-person
2. **Initial review** — 10-20 business days for residential, 15-30 for commercial
3. **Plan corrections** — If required, 5-10 business days per round
4. **Permit issuance** — Upon final approval

Simple projects like roofing or mechanical replacements may be issued in 5-7 days. Complex commercial projects can take 45+ days.

## Denver's Construction Boom: Where the Real Growth Is

Denver's growth is concentrated in several key areas:

**Downtown Denver & LoDo:** The downtown core continues to see massive investment. New office towers, residential lofts, hotels, and mixed-use projects dominate the landscape. The South Platte River development has attracted billions in investment.

**Cherry Creek & Southeast Denver:** This affluent area is experiencing renewal with luxury residential projects, boutique office space, and retail development. Historic renovations are particularly hot here.

**Industrial & Tech Corridors:** Along the I-25 corridor (north and south), industrial and warehouse projects are booming. Tech companies like Google, Amazon, and Facebook have major operations driving nearby development.

**Boulder, Fort Collins & Northern Colorado:** College towns and northern metros are experiencing their own booms with multifamily housing, student housing, and mixed-use development.

**Mountain Communities:** Summit County and mountain resort towns (Vail, Aspen, Boulder) have strong construction pipelines for both residential and hospitality projects.

**Multifamily Housing:** Thousands of apartment units are under construction across the metro to meet housing demand from transplants and young professionals.
