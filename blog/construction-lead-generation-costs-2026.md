# What Do Construction Leads Actually Cost? A Contractor's Breakdown for 2026

You know what nobody in the lead generation industry wants to talk about? The real math.

Not "cost per lead"—that number is almost meaningless by itself. What matters is **cost per acquired customer**. If you pay $30 for a lead but only close 1 in 10, your real cost per customer is $300. And that's before you factor in the time you spent chasing the other 9.

Let's run the actual numbers for every major lead source contractors use in 2026.

## Understanding the Real Cost Formula

Here's the formula that matters:

**Cost Per Customer = (Cost Per Lead) / (Close Rate) + (Time Spent × Your Hourly Value)**

Most contractors value their time at $75-$150/hour. If you spend 30 minutes chasing each lead (calling, estimating, driving to the site), that's $37-$75 in time cost per lead—whether you close it or not.

## Lead Source Cost Breakdown

### Angi (HomeAdvisor)

Angi charges per lead, with prices varying by trade, project size, and market. Here's what contractors reported paying in 2026:

Roofing leads typically run $25-$60 each. Remodeling leads cost $30-$80. HVAC leads come in at $20-$50. Plumbing leads range from $15-$40.

With an average close rate of 8-12% on Angi leads, here's the real math for a roofer paying $45 per lead: At a 10% close rate, you need 10 leads to get 1 customer, costing $450 in lead fees alone. Add 30 minutes of time per lead at $100/hour, and that's another $500 in time. Your actual cost per customer is around $950.

For a $8,000 roofing job, that's nearly 12% of revenue spent on acquisition.

### Thumbtack

Thumbtack charges per quote sent rather than per lead received, giving you more control. Quote costs typically range $15-$50 depending on project size.

The close rate tends to be slightly higher than Angi (10-15%) because you're self-selecting which jobs to quote. For a handyman sending $20 quotes at a 12% close rate: 8-9 quotes to land one job, costing $160-$180 in fees plus time. Your cost per customer is typically $300-$500 depending on your trade.

### Google Ads

Google Ads costs vary enormously by market. In competitive metros, "roofing contractor" clicks run $30-$75. In smaller markets, $10-$25. You also need a good landing page to convert clicks into calls—if your website isn't optimized, you're burning money.

A typical Google Ads funnel for a general contractor: $35 per click, 8% click-to-call rate means $437 per phone inquiry. With a 25% close rate on phone calls, your cost per customer is about $1,750. That works if your average job is $50K+. It doesn't work for a $500 faucet repair.

### Referrals

Referrals remain the lowest-cost lead source, but they come with a hidden cost: you can't turn them on and off. They require years of relationship building, and they plateau. Every contractor hits a ceiling where referrals alone can't sustain growth.

The cost per referral customer is essentially $0 in direct spend, but the time invested in nurturing relationships over years is substantial. Think of referrals as the foundation, not the growth engine.

## What Top Contractors Actually Do

The contractors we see growing fastest don't rely on any single source. They build a **lead stack** that balances cost, control, and consistency:

**Foundation layer (free):** Google Business Profile, referrals, and basic permit alerts. This covers your baseline lead flow at zero cost.

**Growth layer (low cost):** Pro permit monitoring to catch every relevant project in your area, plus targeted social media presence. This runs $50-$150/month total.

**Scale layer (investment):** Google Ads for your highest-margin services, targeted to your best-performing zip codes. Budget $500-$2,000/month and measure ruthlessly.

The key insight: as you move up the stack, each layer should be funded by the wins from the layer below it. Don't start with $2,000/month in Google Ads. Start with free permit alerts, close a few extra jobs, then reinvest.

When you reach out to a property owner who just pulled a $200,000 addition permit, you're not cold-calling. You're offering services to someone who has already committed to a major construction project. That's why permit-sourced leads convert at 2-3x the rate of paid lead platforms.
