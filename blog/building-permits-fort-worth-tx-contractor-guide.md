## Fort Worth's Building Permit System

Fort Worth manages building permits through the **Development Services Department**, which operates from the Bob Bolen Public Safety Complex. The city streamlines permitting by consolidating building, planning, and engineering services in one location, reducing the number of stops needed to get a project approved.

### Where to Apply

**In-Person:** Visit the **Bob Bolen Public Safety Complex at 505 W Felix Street, Fort Worth, TX 76115**. Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM. All permitting services are co-located for efficient one-stop processing.

**Phone Support:** (817) 392-2222 for permits, plan review, and inspections

**Online:** Fort Worth offers electronic plan submission through their development portal. Online submission is increasingly available and recommended for expedited processing.

### Permit Costs in Fort Worth

Fort Worth's fee structure is competitive and reflects the city's business-friendly approach:

- **Residential minor permits (fences, minor work):** $200-$400
- **Standard residential permits:** $300-$600
- **Swimming pool permits:** $150-$500
- **Commercial permits:** $2,000-$15,000+ based on project valuation
- **Electrical, plumbing, mechanical permits:** $100-$500 each
- **Demolition permits:** $250-$800

**Key advantage:** Fort Worth's permit data includes Permit_No, Permit_Type, Permit_SubType, Full_Street_Address, Owner_Full_Name, File_Date, Current_Status, JobValue, Use_Type, and Specific_Use — making it valuable for identifying project types and tracking owner activity across the city.

### Fort Worth's Permit Timeline

Fort Worth's permitting follows an efficient process:

1. **Submission** — Same-day acceptance via online or in-person at Bob Bolen Complex
2. **Plan review** — 5-10 days for minor permits, 10-20 days for standard residential, 15-30 days for commercial
3. **Revision cycles** — 5-10 business days per additional review if corrections needed
4. **Permit issuance** — Upon approval and fee payment
5. **Inspections** — Scheduled via phone at (817) 392-2222

Express review options are available for projects willing to pay expedited review fees, often reducing review time by 30-50%.

## Fort Worth's Construction Market: Where the Growth Is

Fort Worth's construction activity is concentrated in distinct growth corridors and neighborhoods:

**North Fort Worth / Alliance Area:** The hottest growth zone. Major commercial and industrial development near Alliance Airport drives logistics, warehouse, and distribution center construction. Suburban residential growth spreads northward with master-planned communities. Contractors here work on everything from industrial facilities to high-volume residential subdivisions.

**Downtown Fort Worth:** Active mixed-use, commercial, and hospitality development. The Fort Worth skyline continues to grow with office towers, apartments, hotels, and retail mixed-use projects. Fort Worth's downtown renaissance creates consistent work for commercial contractors, MEP specialists, and interior trades.

**Stockyards / North Side:** Historic character mixed with modern development. The Stockyards area sees heritage-sensitive renovation alongside new mixed-use projects. Unique permitting requirements for historically sensitive areas attract specialized contractors.

**Cultural District:** Theater, museum, and arts-focused development. Home to the Kimbell Art Museum, Modern Art Museum, and performing arts venues. Cultural District projects demand specialized construction expertise and attract premium-budget clients.

**Walsh Ranch / Clearfork:** Large mixed-use developments featuring residential, retail, office, and hospitality. These master-planned communities generate consistent construction work across multiple phases and trade types.

**Near Southside / Heritage:** Gentrification and infill development create steady remodeling and new construction. Historic home renovation, adaptive reuse, and new construction for young professionals drive activity.

**Suburban Growth Corridors:** West Fort Worth, Southwest Fort Worth, and Southeast Fort Worth see steady residential subdivision development. Master-planned communities generate high volume of permits across residential trades.
