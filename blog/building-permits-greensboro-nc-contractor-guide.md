## Greensboro's Building Permit System

Greensboro's building permits are managed by the **City of Greensboro Development Services and Inspections Department**. The permitting system covers all residential, commercial, and specialty trade work within the city limits.

### Where to Apply

**In-Person:** Visit the **City of Greensboro Development Services and Inspections Department** during standard business hours. This is the official submission location for all building permits covering Greensboro.

**Online Resources:** The City of Greensboro provides online permit tracking so you can monitor your application status, access permit details, and schedule inspections.

**Phone Support:** Contact the Development Services and Inspections Department for permit questions, requirements, and application status.

### Permit Costs in Greensboro

The City of Greensboro's fee structure reflects the city's diverse construction market:

- **Residential minor permits (fences, minor alterations):** $150-$400
- **New home/residential construction permits:** $300-$700+ based on valuation
- **Commercial permits:** $1,500-$8,000+ based on project scope and valuation
- **Electrical, plumbing, mechanical permits:** $100-$500 each
- **Mixed-use development permits:** Often $3,000-$10,000+ depending on scope

**Key advantage:** Greensboro's permit database includes PermitNum, PermitType, ApplicationType, Contractor, CurrentPermitFee, TotalCost, OwnerName, FullAddress, BuildingSqFt, NumUnits, IssuedDate, CurrentStatus, Description, HistoricDistrict, and Zoning information — making it exceptionally valuable for identifying active projects, tracking premium data like owner names and contractor information, and targeting opportunities.

### Greensboro's Permit Timeline

The City of Greensboro follows a structured permitting process:

1. **Submission** — In-person at Development Services office
2. **Plan review** — 5-15 days for residential minor, 15-30 for new homes, 30-60 for commercial projects
3. **Revision cycles** — 5-10 business days per additional review if corrections needed
4. **Permit issuance** — Upon approval and fee payment
5. **Inspections** — Multiple inspections throughout construction (foundation, framing, mechanical, final)

Projects in Downtown Greensboro or other designated districts may require additional planning review, extending timelines accordingly.
