## St. Paul's Building Permit System

St. Paul's Department of Planning and Economic Development (PED) manages building permits through a **comprehensive ArcGIS platform** that's become a model for municipal transparency.

### Where to Apply

**Online (Recommended):** St. Paul's ArcGIS-based permit portal at **stpaul.gov/permits** is the primary submission method. The system is intuitive, allows 24/7 submissions, and provides real-time status tracking. Account creation takes 15 minutes.

**In-Person:** The Planning and Economic Development office is located at **25 W. 4th St, Suite 1500, St. Paul, MN 55102** (in downtown St. Paul). Hours are Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM. Walk-in service is available but can have wait times.

**Phone Support:** (651) 266-8989 for general inquiries and permit assistance (Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 5 PM)

**Email:** peds@ci.stpaul.mn.us for non-urgent questions

### Permit Costs in St. Paul

St. Paul's permit fees are straightforward and percentage-based on construction valuation:

- **Residential single-family remodel:** $150-$1,200
- **Residential addition:** $300-$1,500
- **Multifamily residential:** $1,500-$10,000
- **Commercial tenant improvement:** $800-$8,000
- **Commercial new construction:** $2,000-$25,000+
- **Plan review fees:** $75-$400
- **Inspection fees:** $50-$150 per inspection

St. Paul publishes detailed [fee schedules by project type](https://www.stpaul.gov/city-services/building-permits). The city's ArcGIS system displays estimated valuations for most permits, so you can quickly compare similar projects and benchmark your own estimates.

### St. Paul's Permit Timeline

St. Paul tracks and publishes average processing times:

1. **Submission** — Immediate online, same-day in-person
2. **Initial review** — 5-15 business days for residential, 10-20 for commercial
3. **Plan corrections** — 3-5 business days per round if needed
4. **Permit issuance** — Upon final approval

Historic preservation review (required in several St. Paul neighborhoods) can add 5-10 business days.

## St. Paul's Construction Boom: Where the Real Growth Is

St. Paul's growth is concentrated in high-opportunity areas:

**Downtown St. Paul & Lowertown:** The downtown core has transformed with major mixed-use development, office conversions, and new residential lofts. Lowertown is experiencing a renaissance with adaptive reuse projects converting historic warehouses into apartments, office, and retail. This is the city's most active sector.

**Green Line Light Rail Corridor:** Along the Green Line (connecting Downtown St. Paul to the airport), major mixed-use development and multifamily housing projects are booming. Developers are concentrating density near transit hubs.

**Historic Districts & Preservation:** St. Paul has generous tax credits and preservation incentives. Contractors specializing in historic renovation and adaptive reuse are seeing consistent demand. Projects like Cathedral Hill, Ramsey Hill, and other historic neighborhoods are being restored.

**Highland Park & South St. Paul:** Residential neighborhoods are experiencing renovation and infill development. Young families relocating from Minneapolis are driving demand for updated homes and new construction.

**Industrial Areas:** Along the Mississippi River and in Northeast St. Paul, industrial facilities, warehouses, and mixed-use industrial projects are under development.

**Healthcare & Institutional:** Expansion projects from major healthcare providers and educational institutions provide consistent, high-value subcontracting work.
